The Great Bear

Thousands of years ago, before satellites and maps, people searched the sky for direction.

They discovered a giant constellation stretching across the heavens — Ursa Major, the Great Bear.

It became a celestial guardian watching over explorers, wanderers, and dreamers.

The Pattern Within

Hidden within Ursa Major lies a smaller pattern of seven bright stars.

This pattern is known today as the Big Dipper.

It is not the entire constellation, but a simple guide hidden within something much larger.

The Traveller's Compass

Two stars in the Big Dipper form a line pointing toward a single light — the North Star.

Unlike every other star, the North Star barely moves.

For centuries sailors, explorers, and lost wanderers followed it to find their way home.
